History of Arrestling

Training to take someone, who is really resisting, into custody

Since 1990 Sergeant Don Gulla and his training partners have developed cutting adge force on forcr training. It began with morning Defensive Tactics workouts with fellow Academy Instructor Rob Bardsley. To keep it real, they wore judogis and gun belts. They quickly become aware of gun sensitivity and positions that expose duty weapons, Some of the best martial arts instructors came to the academy to train with them When the strapped on a gun belt, they would shake their head and say, "boy that really changes everything". They learned, even with all their skill, it only takes a second to be disarmed and shot.
Recruits soon asked to join the workouts. Thirty new young fit recruits started the academy every month and some always wanted to challenge the instructors and their technques. Recruit included Ex Force Recon, navy Seals, delta Force and ranger Personnel. There were Martial artists, Pro Boxers, wrestlers, Jiu jitsu Players, Ex Pro football players, and some just pain nasty guys. VCredit must be given to those recruits and local officers who trained with Don and Rob. Without them Arrestling would not exist. "If you can't make it work on the mat, don't take on the street".
Arrestling Groups now train weekly across Washington State. Some participants drive up to two hours to make workouts. Instructors, Recruits, and local officers, working together, continue to develop, test, and learn new techniques. Don has some of the best Training Partners and Instructors in the world of law Enforcement.
